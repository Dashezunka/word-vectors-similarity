Killer Deception: False Advertising in the Animal Kingdom

And why humans aren’t that different

Image from Needpix

False advertising, by U.S. definition, “misrepresents the nature, characteristics, qualities, or geographic origin of a product or service.”

And animals couldn’t give less of a flying truck about that definition.

Nature’s advertising serves two basic functions:

Signals are solicitations of an individual's relative genetics, fitness, and health. They aren’t always truthful signals.

Why? Because lying — love it or hate it — works.

The stakes are far higher for animals. They aren’t worried about lawsuits and reputation. They lie to live and reproduce — or they die.
